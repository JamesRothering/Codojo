Most engagements start with a direct conversation—no intake form, no scheduling maze. Tell me what you're dealing with and I'll tell you honestly whether I'm the right person for it.

I work best in situations where the environment is complex, the timeline is real, and the stakes are high enough that getting architecture wrong is expensive.

How Engagements Typically Begin

01
A direct email or message. Describe the situation—not the job description. What's stuck, what's at risk, what's the pressure.
02
A short call. Usually 30 minutes. I ask questions that cut to the core of the problem. You'll get something useful out of the call regardless of whether we work together.
03
A scoped review. For larger engagements I typically start with a defined assessment period—enough to form an independent view before committing to a longer arrangement.
04
Ongoing engagement. Fractional CTO retainer, architecture advisory, or project-based. Structured for the situation, not a boilerplate contract.
