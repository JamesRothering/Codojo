Zero-downtime datacenter migration under regulatory pressure

BNP Paribas faced a regulatory deadline for relocating a major datacenter and disaster recovery site. The environment: 54 floors of trading-desk support systems, thousands of interconnected assets, 35 live-mirrored systems, and regulators watching the clock.

"The most efficient facilitator I have ever seen." — Senior Trading Executive, BNP Paribas

I interviewed stakeholders across the organization, built the move methodology, secured executive buy-in, and executed the migration ahead of schedule with zero downtime. The move covered 5,783 pieces of kit and avoided an estimated $10M in regulatory fines.

Every dependency was mapped, every contingency owned, and every communication timed. The result was a migration that no one noticed—because they weren't supposed to.

Stabilizing a production system—and building the path forward

Buró de Crédito México was experiencing three production incidents per week. In the first month, I reviewed the application, infrastructure, and database architecture, identified the root causes, and drove incidents to zero.

I authored a 70-page remediation roadmap covering architectural improvements, operational procedures, and modernization priorities. I also designed a RAG-based anti-fraud system with third-party AML integration and a customer-service portal concept.

The goal wasn't just stability. It was a system that could support the business for the next several years without needing another emergency engagement.
Hulu
Media · Cassandra · AWS · SRE
Peak sustained throughput
1.5M TPS
Super Bowl traffic peak. Hybrid Cassandra on AWS.

1.5 million transactions per second—on the biggest streaming night of the year

The Super Bowl is not a load test you can easily simulate in advance. Hulu's infrastructure needed to handle one of the highest traffic spikes in streaming—and it needed to handle it without anyone noticing.

I optimized Hulu's hybrid Cassandra cluster using AWS auto-scaling scripts and operational tuning, reaching a sustained peak of 1.5 million transactions per second. Deep Cassandra internals knowledge, AWS scaling mechanics, and the operational instinct to tune under live pressure. There is no sandbox for a Super Bowl.
